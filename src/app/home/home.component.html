56 YEARS OF SERVICE TO HUMANITY

In the heart of Jaipur, a celestial marvel emerged from the dreams of Srila Prabhupada. The Krishna-Balaram temple, a testament to the devotion and love of the people, stands as a resplendent jewel.
Its ethereal beauty transcends mortal realms, captivating all who behold it. Within its sacred walls, devotees immerse themselves in the divine bliss of kirtans and arati ceremonies.
Festivals resound with joyous chants and celestial melodies, touching souls with divine grace. Tears flow freely as hearts surrender to the enchanting presence of Sri Sri Radha Madan Mohan Ji. This temple, a haven of spirituality, ignites tears of joy, awakening an eternal love within all who witness its magnificence.

Although our temple has numerous departments performing various different services, the main focus is on the deity worship, prasadam distribution, studying and distributing Srila Prabhupada’s books, goshala, devotee care, and all other functions carried out in the temple are all meant to support and encourage the betterment and success of these key services.

OUR GLORIOUS SPIRITUAL MASTER

A. C. Bhaktivedanta Swami Prabhupada

Srila Prabhupada was born Abhay Charan De on September 1, 1896 to a pious Hindu family in Calcutta. As a youth growing up in British-controlled India, Abhay became involved with Mahatma Gandhi’s civil disobedience movement to secure independence for his nation. It was, however, a 1922 meeting with a prominent scholar and religious leader, Srila Bhaktisiddhanta Sarasvati, which proved most influential on Abhay’s future calling.

One cannot reach the real point of factual knowledge without being helped by the right person who is already established in that knowledge. There.

In 1965, at the age of sixty-nine, Srila Prabhupada traveled to New York City aboard a cargo ship. The journey was treacherous, and the elderly spiritual teacher suffered two heart attacks aboard ship.

On July 11, 1966, he officially registered his organization in the state of New York, formally founding the International Society for Krishna Consciousness.
